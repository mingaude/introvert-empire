From An Introvert To Extrovert: How To Become An Introvert And Enjoy It

A small collection of ideas and insights on how to own quarantine if you're an extrovert

As an introvert born and raised, I feel it's time that I share my experience and knowledge in these difficult times of the COVID-19 crisis. Here you will find small tips and tasks how to make your prolonged quarantine an enjoyable experience – a canvas for growth and creative ideas.

King in the kingdom, not prisoner in the prison

Your home is not your prison, it's your kingdom and you reign over all and everything that encompasses it. Well, except for the other co-owners. Maybe you even have a democracy. Happens. In any case, it is the space where you can run around stark naked, build your own fortress and paint a giant mural of Yoko Ono on the wall.

What I'm basically trying to convey is that you're not trapped in your home with no escape. And your home isn't a cell. It's your space, open for creation and freedom of your action choice. Here you matter, your enjoyment, your well-being, your state of mind, your busyness or boredom. It is essential to start seeing your home as your own space for creativity, for only then can you truly enter a relaxed state of mind and start filling your time with enjoyable, meaningful things.
